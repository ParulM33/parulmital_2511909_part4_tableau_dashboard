# Chart Selection Justification

 

## 1. Sales Trend Over Time

 

### Business Question

How are sales changing throughout the year?

 

### Chart Type Selected

Line Chart

 

### Why This Chart Is Appropriate

A line chart is the most effective visualization for displaying trends and changes over time. It allows leadership to easily identify seasonal patterns, peaks, declines, and overall sales movement across months.

 

### Fields Used

- X-Axis: Order Month

- Y-Axis: Sales

 

### Design Principle Applied

- Chronological ordering

- Minimal clutter

- Consistent color usage

- Clear trend visualization

 

### Mistake Avoided

Avoided using a bar chart or pie chart, which would make trend analysis more difficult and reduce readability.

 

---

 

## 2. Regional Performance View

 

### Business Question

Which regions contribute the most sales?

 

### Chart Type Selected

Horizontal Bar Chart

 

### Why This Chart Is Appropriate

A horizontal bar chart provides a clear comparison between regions and allows quick identification of top-performing and lower-performing regions.

 

### Fields Used

- Y-Axis: Region

- X-Axis: Sales

- Labels: Total Sales

 

### Design Principle Applied

- Sorted descending order

- Direct value labeling

- Easy comparison across categories

 

### Mistake Avoided

Avoided pie charts and maps. Pie charts make comparison difficult, while maps prioritize geography over precise sales comparison.

 

---

 

## 3. Category and Sub-Category Profitability

 

### Business Question

Which categories and sub-categories generate the highest profit?

 

### Chart Type Selected

Horizontal Bar Chart

 

### Why This Chart Is Appropriate

The chart enables comparison of profitability across both category and sub-category levels while highlighting the strongest profit contributors.

 

### Fields Used

- Category

- Sub-Category

- Profit

- Color by Category

 

### Design Principle Applied

- Hierarchical grouping

- Consistent category colors

- Direct profit labels

- Clear ranking

 

### Mistake Avoided

Avoided collapsing analysis to category level only, which would hide important insights about high-performing sub-categories such as Copiers, Accessories, and Phones.

 

---

 

## 4. Customer Segment Analysis

 

### Business Question

How does profitability compare across customer segments?

 

### Chart Type Selected

Horizontal Bar Chart

 

### Why This Chart Is Appropriate

Bar charts effectively compare profitability metrics across a small number of customer segments and allow leadership to identify differences quickly.

 

### Fields Used

- Customer Segment

- Sales

- Profit Margin

 

### Design Principle Applied

- Percentage labels displayed directly

- Consistent color palette

- Easy segment comparison

 

### Mistake Avoided

Avoided overly complex visualizations that would not improve understanding for only three customer segments.

 

---

 

## 5. Return Rate by Category

 

### Business Question

Which product categories experience the highest product returns?

 

### Chart Type Selected

Horizontal Bar Chart

 

### Why This Chart Is Appropriate

Return rates are best compared using bars because differences between categories can be interpreted quickly and accurately.

 

### Fields Used

- Category

- Return Rate

 

### Design Principle Applied

- Descending order ranking

- Percentage labels

- Clear visual contrast

 

### Mistake Avoided

Avoided pie charts because small differences in return percentages would be difficult to compare accurately.

 

---

 

## 6. Average Delivery Days by Ship Mode

 

### Business Question

Which shipping methods require the longest delivery time?

 

### Chart Type Selected

Vertical Bar Chart

 

### Why This Chart Is Appropriate

A bar chart provides an effective comparison of average delivery days across shipping methods and highlights operational differences.

 

### Fields Used

- Ship Mode

- Average Delivery Days

 

### Design Principle Applied

- Direct value labels

- Simple layout

- Operational performance focus

 

### Mistake Avoided

Avoided line charts because shipping methods are categories rather than time-based variables.

 

---

 

## 7. Discount Impact Analysis

 

### Business Question

How do discounts relate to profitability across product categories?

 

### Chart Type Selected

Scatter Plot

 

### Why This Chart Is Appropriate

Scatter plots are specifically designed to explore relationships between two numerical measures. This visualization allows leadership to compare average discount levels against average profit levels for each category.

 

### Fields Used

- X-Axis: Average Discount

- Y-Axis: Average Profit

- Labels: Category

 

### Design Principle Applied

- Relationship-focused visualization

- Minimal clutter

- Direct category labeling

- Clear comparison of discount and profit relationship

 

### Mistake Avoided

Avoided bar charts because they would not effectively demonstrate the relationship between discounting and profitability.

 

---

 

## Dashboard Design Principles

 

The dashboard was designed using the following visualization best practices:

 

- Consistent color usage across views

- Clear KPI placement at the top of the dashboard

- Logical storytelling flow from sales performance to profitability, risk, and operational analysis

- Minimal chart clutter

- Readable chart titles and labels

- Appropriate use of business-friendly formats such as currency and percentages

- Interactive dashboard filters for exploratory analysis

- Focus on actionable insights rather than decorative visuals

 

### Dashboard Story Flow

 

The dashboard guides users through a logical business narrative:

 

1. Overall business performance through KPI metrics.

2. Sales performance over time.

3. Regional sales contribution.

4. Category and sub-category profitability.

5. Customer segment profitability.

6. Return-related business risks.

7. Shipping performance analysis.

8. Discount impact on profitability.

 

This structure supports executive decision-making and enables leadership to quickly identify opportunities and risks
